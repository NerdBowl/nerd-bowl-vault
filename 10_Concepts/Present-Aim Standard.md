### Present-Aim Standard
A person is [[Rational Choice Theory|rational]] under the present-aim standard if they are efficient in the pursuit of whatever aims they happen to hold at the moment of action. This standard imposes no normative constraints on the aims themselves; it only assesses the efficiency of the means used to achieve them.
