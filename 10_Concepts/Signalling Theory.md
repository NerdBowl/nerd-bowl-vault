### Signalling Theory
Signalling Theory addresses [[Asymmetric Information|asymmetric information]] by allowing the informed party (Agent) to transmit a visible signal to the uninformed party ([[Principal-Agent Problem|Principal]]) to reveal private information. For a signal to be effective in a non-cooperative environment, it must reliably distinguish between types of agents.
